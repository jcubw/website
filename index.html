About Me

Jacob Wang is a highly accomplished pianist known for his exceptional performances, insightful teaching, and collaborative endeavors. Jacob is a graduate of the University of Michigan where he studied under the guidance of Dr. Arthur Greene, Dr. John Ellis, and Dr. Aya Hagelthorn. His notable performances include a captivating rendition of Brahms' Concerto in D with the Raleigh Symphony Orchestra, a sold-out Candlelight Concert in Ann Arbor, and an engaging chamber music concert in Bayside, ME. Jacob's artistry shines through in diverse settings, from solo recitals in Wilmington, NC, to a duo recital with a violinist during a Germany tour.

As a passionate educator, Jacob serves on the faculty of the Piano Pedagogy Laboratory Program at the University of Michigan, where he imparts his knowledge through individual and group lessons. He is not only a skilled performer but also a committed advocate for music education, having presented at the National Conference of Music Teacher National Association and contributing to the MTNA UM Chapter as Treasurer.

In addition to his performance and teaching endeavors, Jacob has been actively involved in innovative projects that demonstrate his leadership and organizational skills. One notable project includes his role as the Executive Director of the Candlelight Concert, a unique event where 15 pianists publicly speak and perform amidst over 700 candles. His adept management resulted in a sold-out concert, showcasing his ability to navigate complex logistics and successfully lead a team. As Jacob Wang continues his musical journey, he remains dedicated to the art form, seeking opportunities for collaboration and education with a humble and genuine passion for sharing the beauty of music.
